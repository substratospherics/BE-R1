Why is blue so rare in nature?

REASON 1

Due to their difficulty in synthesis and lower importance for survival and reproduction than other colors, blue pigments like anthocyanins and delphinidins are less common in plants and animals than other hues.

  • These blue pigments are frequently generated as secondary metabolites, which means they are not required for fundamental metabolic functions and may have evolved for particular goals like luring pollinators or discouraging herbivores. Blue pigments are also regularly produced in response to environmental pressures like UV radiation, rather than being a persistent characteristic.
  • Contrarily, pigments like chlorophyll in plants and carotenoids in animals are more often found since they are required for photosynthesis and visual communication, respectively.
  • To explain, photosynthesis—the process by which plants turn light energy into chemical energy in the form of glucose—requires chlorophyll, the green pigment found in plants. Chlorophyll reflects green light and gives plants their characteristic color by absorbing light mostly in the blue and red spectrums. Chlorophyll pigments are located in chloroplasts, which are organelles unique to plant cells that are used for photosynthesis. Chlorophyll is present in nearly all plants since photosynthesis is essential for their life and development.
  • On the other hand, there exist pigments known as carotenoids that are found in the skin, feathers, and scales of animals and are responsible for their yellow, orange, and red hues. These pigments do not contribute to photosynthesis, but they do help animals communicate visually by attracting potential partners, protecting territory, and blending in with their surroundings. Carotenoids also have added benefits for animals, as they act as protectors of cells by neutralizing harmful molecules referred to as free radicals.
  • As a result, chlorophyll and carotenoids are each crucial pigments for their respective organisms. Chlorophyll is required for photosynthesis, which is vital for plant survival and growth, whereas carotenoids are required for visible communication and have antioxidant houses. As a result, they are more prominent but not uncommon than blue pigments.
